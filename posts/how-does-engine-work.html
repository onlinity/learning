How does engine work?

In modern world, traveling is simpier than it ever was. We have access to many different vehicles, each having its own advantages over others. One thing all those have in common is - the engine. There are many kinds of engines, but we will explain the one which can be found in cars.

Engine parts

Engine is obviously really complex, but the principle behind its work is relatively simple. But first, let's see what it contains.
Engine can be divided into 2 main parts - block and head, with head gasket in between. Block contains several pistons and cylinders (usually 4 or 6), and head holds valves, fuel injection and ignition systems, all needed for pistons' work.

Parts of an engine. Credits: https://www.carthrottle.com/post/head-gasket-failure-check-yourself-before-you-wreck-yourself/

Work process

You may have heard of "four-stroke" - term used to describe cycle of engine's piston.
Step 1 - Intake. Piston moves down, creating vacuum, and pulling air in through intake valves. Fuel is added into the air, creating highly flamable mixture.
Step 2 - Compression. Piston moves up, compressing air and fuel.
Step 3 - Power. Ignition system creates a spark, igniting fuel and starting a controlled explosion. Explosion pushed piston back down.
Step 4 - Exhaust. While piston goes up, it pushes used gas into exhaust pipes and out of the vehicle.
When piston moves (during all 4 stages), it rotates internal shaft (crankshaft), which rotates vehicles wheels or propellers.

Four-stroke cycle. Credits: https://www.britannica.com/technology/four-stroke-cycle

Engine types

Depending on the position of the pistons, there are couple of engine designes. When all pistons are arranged in a row, it is a inline engine. If pistons are grouped into 2 banks connected at an angle, creating a "V" shape, it is a V-engine. Finally, when angle between these banks is 180°, that is a boxer (flat) engine.
